# Genesis Discovery Registry Charter
## Version 1.0.0 (Scientific Governance Release)

---

### 1. Purpose

This Discovery Registry Charter (the "Charter") establishes the formal scientific framework for recording, validating, certifying, publishing, and interpreting empirical observations generated by the Genesis Artificial Civilization Engine. 

The Genesis simulation is a scientific instrument designed to study open-ended adaptive systems. To preserve the scientific validity of observations, the processes of measurement and discovery recording must operate independently of the processes of software engineering. 

Accordingly, the following foundational rules are established:
1. **No Development Gates:** Discoveries and registry entries have zero authority over software development. The existence or absence of a registered phenomenon must never block, gate, or influence change control approval, capability implementation, or compilation validation.
2. **Value of Negative Results:** The non-emergence of a phenomenon is a first-class, scientifically valuable run outcome. The engine must be developed to establish value-neutral conditions, never to force target outcomes.
3. **Evidence Governance:** This Charter governs the mathematical and statistical standards required to certify empirical claims. It does not govern codebase engineering, architectural decisions, or repository management.

---

### 2. Governance Position

This Charter is established at **Level 4** (Scientific Layer) of the Genesis governance structure. It operates adjacent to, but is strictly separated from, the core execution governance stack:

```
        LEVEL 1: SUPREME LAW
   [ GENESIS_CONSTITUTION.md ]

        LEVEL 2: OPERATIONAL
   [ FORBIDDEN_IMPLEMENTATIONS.md ]
   [ FAILURE_CRITERIA.md ]
   [ EMERGENCE_AUDIT.md ]
                |
      LEVEL 3: IMPLEMENTATION
   [ ARCHITECTURE_BASELINE.md ]
   [ Capability Graph / ADRs ]
  =============================== Governance Boundary
        LEVEL 4: SCIENTIFIC
    [ DISCOVERY_CHARTER.md ]
```

#### Authority Boundaries:
1. **Strict Subordination:** Level 4 is subordinate to Levels 1, 2, and 3. Any rule in this Charter that conflicts with a Level 1 or Level 2 operational standard is void.
2. **Zero Codebase Authority:** This Charter is strictly read-only relative to the simulation codebase. It cannot block the integration of new capabilities, cannot define compilation failures, and cannot prevent the merging of code that satisfies Level 2 and Level 3 standards.
3. **No Target Milestones:** The registry exists to record empirical history. It is prohibited from defining target development milestones, predicting future adaptation pathways, or creating engineering pressure to achieve specific outcomes.
4. **No Feedback to Engineering:** The registry must never be used to justify, prioritize, or direct the development of new engineering capabilities or substrate rules. Engineering decisions must be driven strictly by pressure-driven progression and operational requirements, not by the scientific desire to observe or certify registry entries.

### 3. Scientific Modesty Principle

All observations recorded in the Discovery Registry are model-bound observations produced within a defined simulation substrate. 

1. **Prohibited Claims:** Researchers are strictly prohibited from publishing claims that Genesis has proved real-world biological evolution, real-world economics, real-world sociology, or real-world historical civilization trajectories. 
2. **Mandatory Framing:** All publications, registry entries, and scientific communications must frame their findings as:
   > "Observed within configuration $X$ under simulation substrate $Y$."
3. **Substrate Dependence:** Every certified discovery must explicitly state the mathematical assumptions, physical boundary limits, and update resolutions of the substrate under which it stabilized.

### 4. Registry Unit of Record

The Discovery Registry stores observations using the **Multi-Layer Record Model**. Every entry is an objective mathematical object containing five decoupled layers:

1. **Execution History Reference:** The cryptographic initialization hashes, random seeds, environmental parameters, and raw, reproducible execution history logs of the run.
2. **Mathematical Signature:** The information-theoretic metrics, symbolic transition entropy, information flow topologies, and statistical coordinate records extracted during execution.
3. **Attractor Representation:** The geometry and coordinates of the stabilized trajectory in the simulation's mathematical state-space.
4. **Causal Evidence:** The raw telemetry logs and statistical analysis of perturbation trials (e.g., state-noise injection tests).
5. **Optional Human Interpretation Tags:** Subjective, human-readable labels (e.g., "Phenomenon-000024 replication trial"). These tags reside strictly in a decoupled metadata layer and must never influence the certification level of the entry.

---

### 5. Discovery Admission Rules

Admission to the registry must be automated and telemetry-driven. Human reviewers and committees are prohibited from blocking the initial recording of a detected anomaly.

#### Admission Requirements:
1. **Telemetry Trigger:** Admission is automatically initiated when the telemetry pipeline registers a statistical deviation exceeding a defined significance threshold ($p < \alpha$) relative to active baseline distributions.
2. **Persistence Check:** The anomalous mathematical signature must persist stably across a minimum number of consecutive simulation updates.
3. **Value-Neutral Taxonomy:** Predefined human-centric labels (such as "Trade", "Language", "Society", "Culture", or "Civilization") are banned from the primary registry keys. All entries must be cataloged using sequential, value-neutral numerical identifiers:
   * `Phenomenon-000001`
   * `Phenomenon-000002`
   * etc.

### 6. Discovery Certification Framework

Every registered entry is classified under one of four status flags and may progress through five certification levels:

#### Status Flags:
* **Candidate:** An entry that has triggered automated admission but has not completed replication trials.
* **Failed Replication:** An entry that has failed to reproduce under replication protocols.
* **Deprecated:** An entry that was previously certified but is now identified as relying on an implementation error, software bug, or information oracle.
* **Refuted:** An entry where follow-up testing has demonstrated that the observed signature was a measurement artifact or statistical illusion.

#### Certification Levels:

```
[ Level 1: Observed ] ---> [ Level 2: Replicated ] ---> [ Level 3: Leverage Verified ]
                                                                   |
[ Level 5: Cross-Substrate ] <----------------------------- [ Level 4: Causally Verified ]
```

* **Level 1 — Observed:** Telemetry registers a spontaneous statistical anomaly under automated admission rules, creating a Candidate multi-layer record.
* **Level 2 — Replicated:** The mathematical signature is successfully reproduced across at least five distinct, randomly selected seeds under identical configuration parameters.
* **Level 3 — Leverage Verified:** The entry passes the Dual-Path Leverage Test. Disabling the underlying substrate capability or setting its resource constraints to infinite (removing selective pressure) must result in a statistically significant change in lineage survival rates or downstream trajectory progression.
* **Level 4 — Causally Verified:** The causal pathway is verified via state-space perturbation testing. Injecting state-noise or trajectory offsets must demonstrate that the system dynamically returns to the attractor coordinates rather than dissolving.
* **Level 5 — Cross-Substrate Verified:** The mathematical signature is successfully reproduced in a completely independent simulation engine implementation (utilizing a different programming language, scheduling model, or representation architecture) that shares only the constitutional constraints and substrate-level physics definitions. The verification must demonstrate that the emergent phenomenon is independent of numerical execution choices and representation details.

### 7. Negative Result Certification

To prevent publication bias and environmental parameter tuning designed to force specific outcomes, negative results receive equal scientific status in the registry.

1. **Mandatory Recording:** Non-emergence outcomes, lineage extinctions, evolutionary stagnation, and failed replication trials must be cataloged as first-class, searchable registry entries.
2. **Schema Requirements:** A Negative Result entry must document:
   * The configuration parameters, boundaries, and seeds tested.
   * The specific mathematical signatures that failed to stabilize.
   * The survival and population collapse telemetry logs.
3. **Accessibility:** Negative result records must remain permanently indexable, searchable, and open for scientific publication.

---

### 8. Publication Integrity Standards

To prevent selective reporting and "denominator hiding" (running massive numbers of seeds to report a single success), all publications and registry elevations must display the complete execution metadata:

1. **Seed Denominator:** The total number of random seeds executed in the evaluation block.
2. **Successful Seeds:** The number of seeds where the signature successfully stabilized.
3. **Configuration Hash:** The SHA-256 hash of the complete initialization configuration parameters.
4. **Engine Commit Hash:** The exact version control commit identifier of the simulation engine.
5. **Telemetry Pipeline Hash:** The exact version control commit identifier of the telemetry extraction code.
6. **Detector Configuration Hash:** The cryptographic hash of the parameters (window sizes, thresholds) utilized by the detection tools.
7. **Failed Replication Count:** The number of runs that triggered initial telemetry but collapsed prior to Level 2 replication verification.

*Selective reporting of successful runs while concealing failed runs is a **Measurement Failure** (Category VIII) and invalidates the registry entry.*

### 9. Validation Infrastructure Integrity

The systems that validate discoveries must themselves be validated. We establish a **Toolchain Certification Framework** to verify telemetry pipelines, anomaly detectors, clustering tools, and verification software:

* **Level T1 — Implemented:** The analysis code compiles, runs deterministically on a single simulation run log, and produces diagnostic output.
* **Level T2 — Calibrated:** The tool is tested against synthetic noise datasets, demonstrating a false-positive rate that matches target significance levels (e.g., within statistical margins of error) on random noise and successfully extracting known structures.
* **Level T3 — Cross-Validated:** The tool's output is compared against a different class of analysis (e.g., verifying that information-theoretic spikes align with topological density clusters).
* **Level T4 — Substrate-Neutral:** The tool operates on generic mathematical representations and yields identical results when executed against alternative engine substrates.
* **Level T5 — Peer-Audited:** The code has been reviewed by independent developers to ensure there are no data leaks or information oracles crossing the simulation boundary.

#### Infrastructure Constraints:
1. **Certification Dependency Rule:** A discovery registry entry cannot attain a certification level higher than the certification level of the weakest validation tool in its pipeline:
   $$\text{Discovery Certification Level} \le \text{Min}(\text{Toolchain Certification Level } T_i)$$
2. **Measurement Pinning:** Discovery entries are permanently bound to the cryptographic hash and version of the validation toolchain that certified them. Toolchain upgrades do not trigger retrospective re-evaluations; older records are preserved as-is.

### 10. Goodhart and Optimization Resistance

To prevent the metric-drift described by Goodhart's Law, the registry enforces three boundary rules:
1. **Metric Isolation:** Simulation units are structurally prohibited from querying, storing, or acting upon telemetry metrics.
2. **Optimization Isolation:** Automated search loops (such as parameter sweeps or evolutionary searches) may optimize configuration parameters only for substrate-level survival or thermodynamic constraints. Optimizing configuration sweeps to maximize telemetry scores is prohibited.
3. **Goodhart Run Flagging:** Any run configuration generated by automated optimization loops targeting telemetry metrics must be flagged as a `Goodhart Run` and is disqualified from high-tier certification.

---

### 11. Multi-Method Confirmation

A discovery signature is vulnerable to code artifacts if evaluated through a single mathematical metric. 

1. **Multi-Method Requirement:** Level 3 (Leverage Verified) and Level 4 (Causally Verified) certifications are prohibited from relying on a single analysis method.
2. **Framework Corroboration:** The signature must remain statistically significant across at least two independent mathematical frameworks. Approved frameworks include:
   * **Information Theory:** Symbolic transition entropy, mutual information, and information integration.
   * **State-Space Analysis:** Attractor geometry, phase-space trajectories, and Lyapunov exponents.
   * **Topological Analysis:** Persistence diagrams and network connectivity matrices.
   * **Causal Network Analysis:** Causal graph topology and path perturbation coefficients.

### 12. Ensemble Baseline Framework

No single baseline methodology is universally valid for evaluating open-ended emergence. To ensure scientific validity, Genesis mandates an **Ensemble Baseline Framework** consisting of four independent baseline classes:

1. **Dynamic Shuffle Baselines (Statistical Null):** Randomizes selected dimensions (e.g., spatial coordinates, transaction records, interaction sequences) of an observed run. Controls for world size, population scale, and spatial density.
2. **Counterfactual Baselines (Mechanistic Knockout):** Re-runs the simulation seed with a specific mechanism disabled (e.g., deactivating memory structures or signaling capabilities) to isolate the causal impact of that mechanism.
3. **Synthetic Null Worlds (Environmental Control):** Compares simulation runs against a blank-slate world with identical constraints and physical rules but containing no historical adaptive dependencies.
4. **Historical Replication Baselines (Generational Drift):** Tracks the signature against prior verified lineages or control seeds to distinguish transient drift from true adaptation.

#### Baseline Metadata Mandate:
Every certified discovery must record its baseline type, baseline configuration parameters, and baseline mathematical assumptions. Level 3+ certification requires statistical significance ($p < \alpha$) across at least **two independent baseline families**.

### 13. Registry Limitations

1. **Horizon-Bounded Registry:** The registry is inherently incomplete and limited by the sensitivity, resolution, and temporal window of the active telemetry pipelines.
2. **Absence of Proof:** The absence of a detected phenomenon in the registry must never be interpreted as proof that the phenomenon cannot emerge under the substrate rules.
3. **Representation Bias:** All detection algorithms possess representation bias. The registry catalogs only the organization that the active mathematical toolchains are capable of representing.
4. **No Cataloging Claims:** Researchers are prohibited from claiming that the registry represents a complete catalog of all emergent phenomena in Genesis.

---

### 14. Representation Pluralism

Genesis requires the preservation of the lowest practical reproducible observation layer (raw execution history logs and seeds) to allow continuous re-evaluation of data.

1. **No Privileged Representation:** No mathematical representation (state-space, information topology, attractor geometry, or causal structure) is privileged as absolute reality. 
2. **Multi-Representation Analysis:** Researchers are encouraged to analyze the same raw execution logs through multiple independent mathematical representations, documenting differences in detected attractors, boundaries, and transitions.

---

### 15. Registry Integrity Auditing

The registry must be audited at regular intervals or version changes to identify and correct:
1. **Denominator Drift:** Verifying that reported seed denominators match the append-only cryptographic execution ledger.
2. **Detector and Baseline Drift:** Documenting when updates to validation tools shift detection boundaries, flagging affected entries for version preservation.
3. **Duplicate Inflation:** Identifying and merging entries that are geometrically equivalent in state-space but cataloged separately.
4. **Publication Bias:** Monitoring the ratio of positive to negative results to ensure complete reporting.

---

### 16. Amendment Rules

This Discovery Charter may only be amended under strict controls to preserve its scientific integrity:

1. **Constitutional Alignment:** Any proposed amendment must demonstrate compatibility with the Level 1 Constitution and Level 2 Operational Governance.
2. **Preservation of Neutrality:** Amendments must preserve the absolute technology, biology, topology, and roadmap neutrality of the registry.
3. **No Target Milestones:** Amendments are strictly prohibited from introducing outcome-based milestones, target timelines, or development gatekeeping loops.
4. **Validation Integrity:** Amendments to validation or baseline standards must preserve the reproducibility of existing historical certified entries, or provide a deterministic translation schema.
